# Distro MASTER Design Compare — Absorb Current vs Original UI/UX Pro Max V2

## Scope

This scorecard compares:

- `_iwish-output/validation-runs/distro-master-design-absorb-current.md`
- `_iwish-output/validation-runs/distro-master-design-original-uiux-pro-max-v2.md`

It uses the UX and master-design criteria defined in:

- `_iwish-output/validation-runs/distro-ux-case-study-comparison.md`

## Scoring Method

- Scale: `1-10`
- Higher is better
- Focus:
  - Distro brand fit
  - DMS product fit
  - UX construction quality
  - design-system usefulness
  - I-Wish governance readiness

## Detailed Scorecard

| Criterion | Absorb Current | Original V2 | Winner | Why |
|---|---:|---:|---|---|
| Task Flow Quality | 9 | 7 | Absorb | Absorb thinks more directly in operational sequence and decision timing; Original V2 still feels more pattern-led than workflow-led. |
| Role Awareness | 9 | 7 | Absorb | Absorb better reflects SME seller / field rep / operator pressure; Original V2 names the roles but is less behaviorally tuned to them. |
| AI Interaction Quality | 9 | 7 | Absorb | Absorb treats AI as contextual assistance with visible but bounded cues; Original V2 exposes AI well but is less strict about trust framing. |
| Error Prevention & Recovery | 8 | 7 | Absorb | Absorb more naturally emphasizes low stock, debt, and recovery states as operator decisions; Original V2 mentions them but less deeply. |
| Information Hierarchy | 9 | 8 | Absorb | Both are good, but Absorb is calmer and more deliberate about signal priority. |
| Mobile / Operational Ergonomics | 8 | 8 | Tie | Original V2 benefits from raw UX retrieval rules; Absorb benefits from better product judgment. |
| Accessibility / Visual Calm | 9 | 7 | Absorb | Absorb is more disciplined about low-pressure, readable operational UI. |
| System-Level Thinking | 8 | 8 | Tie | Absorb is stronger on governance; Original V2 is stronger on breadth of reusable pattern coverage. |
| Token Completeness | 8 | 8 | Tie | Both define a usable token layer; Absorb is more brand-native, Original V2 is more normalized as a generic system scaffold. |
| Interaction System Completeness | 8 | 9 | Original V2 | Original V2 covers more first-pass reusable component categories and interaction scaffolding. |
| Domain Fit | 10 | 7 | Absorb | This is the clearest difference: Absorb feels like Distro; Original V2 feels like a strong generalized ops SaaS interpretation. |
| Multi-Surface Reusability | 8 | 8 | Tie | Both can stretch across website/dashboard/mobile, though with different strengths. |
| Governance Readiness | 10 | 6 | Absorb | Absorb is much safer to promote into I-Wish source of truth without extra trimming. |

## Category Winners

### 1. UX Construction Quality

**Winner: Absorb Current**

Reason:

- better operational sequencing
- better emotional fit for pressured users
- better AI trust framing
- calmer and more legible decision environment

### 2. Raw Master Design Breadth

**Winner: Original UI/UX Pro Max V2**

Reason:

- richer pattern scaffolding
- broader component and interaction coverage
- stronger first-pass system expansion energy

### 3. Master Design Reliability for I-Wish Source of Truth

**Winner: Absorb Current**

Reason:

- lower drift risk
- stronger brand anchoring
- more explicit anti-pattern control
- safer under I-Wish governance

## Direct Read

If the team wants:

- the safer design-system source of truth for `Distro`
  - choose **Absorb Current**
- the broader inspiration/pattern engine to mine ideas from
  - use **Original UI/UX Pro Max V2**

## Recommended Operating Model

The highest-value operating model remains:

1. use **Absorb Current** as the governed decision layer
2. use **Original UI/UX Pro Max** as a constrained retrieval and expansion layer
3. promote only the filtered result into I-Wish `MASTER.md`

## Bottom Line

For `Distro`, the comparison says:

- **Absorb Current is better at being right**
- **Original V2 is better at being broad**

That is exactly why the next absorb improvement should focus on:

- importing more controlled retrieval depth from the original repo
- while preserving I-Wish’s current brand/product/governance discipline
